Moby is my first solar roller and runs on a 1381-based solar engine using a 1381J, 2N3904, 2N3906, a 2.2 kΩ resistor, and a 0.33 F capacitor. The 1381J triggers at approximately 2.97 V across the storage capacitor. The entire solar engine was free-formed as in Free forming the 1381 SE

The 1381 IC, solar cell, 0.33 F capacitors, and pager motors used in this build were provided by a supporter in the US who preferred to remain anonymous.

1381s are still difficult to find locally, so most builders in India may need to rely on FLEDs (flashing LEDs) instead. FLEDs are available in some electronics shops—I found them in Chennai. Pager motors are the same type used in older Mobi pagers and can sometimes be found in second-hand markets, though removing the counterweight from the shaft can be tricky. High-value capacitors like 0.33 F and 1 F are available in Chennai, though they tend to be relatively expensive.

Solar cells from old calculators can be reused, and wiring them in series or parallel lets you tune the output depending on the requirement—series for higher voltage, parallel for higher current. In this build, however, the primary solar cell was an imported unit.
