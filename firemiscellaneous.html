Final words

FIRE has many flaws which needs to be fixed:

  1. There are no sensors at the back. This has been a major disadvantage considering the size of the robot, and it struggles to get out of room corners.
  2. There is no protection for the circuit boards from pointed obstacles.
  3. The L298 motor driver is inefficient. I should have used the LMD18298 instead.
  4. Five out of six boards were etched at home, which required a significant amount of effort. I had to build the IR board on a general-purpose board because the circuit was too complex. For some reason, I couldn’t bring myself to rely on software tools to design my own PCB layouts, although EAGLE is a solid editor.
  5. I should have searched for better motors with shafts aligned to the motor’s axis.
  6. The robot needs more functionality. Two sensors are not enough. I’m considering adding sonar sensors and replacing the camera with a low-resolution vision system. I also want to add computer control using FM and DTMF. With these additions, the robot will finally feel complete—sometime in the future.
